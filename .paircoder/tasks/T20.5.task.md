# T20.5 — Parts Runner

Create `engine/parts_runner.py` — the sandbox that calls all 10 of your car's part functions every tick. Feeds physical conditions to each part, takes their decisions, feeds those into the physics engines (T20.2-4), and produces the car's behavior.

## Per-tick: your code runs inside this loop

1. Your `engine_map` gets called → physics computes actual torque
2. Your `gearbox` gets called → physics selects the gear ratio
3. Your `fuel_mix` gets called → physics adjusts power and consumption
4. Your `suspension` gets called → physics computes ride height and downforce
5. Your `cooling` gets called → physics applies drag penalty and temp reduction
6. Your `brake_bias` gets called → physics splits braking force front/rear
7. Your `ers_deploy`/`ers_harvest` get called → physics manages battery
8. Your `differential` gets called → physics splits torque between wheels
9. Your `strategy` gets called → pit wall decisions applied

## Part Call Log (for the live terminal UI)

Every call logged: part name, inputs, YOUR output, physics result, status (ok/warning/error).

## Error Handling

If your code crashes, the default takes over. Your car still runs — just slower.

5 tests in `tests/test_parts_runner.py`.
