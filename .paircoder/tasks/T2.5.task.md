# Objective

Replace circle car markers with realistic top-down race car models featuring body, cockpit, rear wing, 4 wheels with steering, shadows, brake lights, and boost effects.

# Implementation Plan

1. Create `viewer/js/car-renderer.js` with `renderCar(ctx, car, prevCar, replay, transform)` function
2. Car body: elongated rounded rectangle (~12 world units long, ~5 wide), rotated to match `track_headings[seg]`, filled with car's color
3. Nose cone: tapered front end (narrower than body)
4. Cockpit: small darker oval/rectangle in upper third of body
5. Rear wing: thin dark rectangle at back of car
6. 4 wheels: small dark rectangles at four corners. Front pair rotated based on heading difference between current seg and next seg (steering angle)
7. Shadow: dark semi-transparent ellipse offset slightly below-right of car body
8. Brake lights: two small red dots at rear, glow when `speed` < `prevCar.speed` (deceleration)
9. Boost effect: subtle warm radial gradient (orange/yellow) behind car exhaust area, not cartoon fire
10. Position number: small white text centered on car body
11. Name label: above car, slightly offset

# Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Cars are clearly identifiable as top-down race cars (not circles)
- [ ] Front wheels visibly turn in corners (steering angle from heading delta)
- [ ] Shadow visible beneath each car
- [ ] Brake lights activate on deceleration (red glow at rear)
- [ ] Boost produces subtle exhaust glow (not cartoonish)
- [ ] Position number readable on car body
- [ ] Car name label visible above car
- [ ] Proper scale: car body ~2-3% of track width
- [ ] Cars rotate smoothly following track heading

# Verification

- Build viewer, load replay, verify car shapes and rotation
- Watch a corner section — verify front wheels turn
- Watch boost activation — verify subtle glow effect
- Watch braking zones — verify brake lights
